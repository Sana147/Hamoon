Background

Over the years, the Pakistani citizens have faced several threats from the state, non-state, and hybrid actors. The state actors include law enforcement, local police, and Internet Service Providers (ISP)s.

State Actors

State actors primarily include law enforcement agencies, local police, and Internet Service Providers (ISPs). A notable example of their actions occurred during the May 9, 2023 riots, when law enforcement employed geo-fencing and geo-tagging technology to track at-risk individuals at protests and rallies. By cross-referencing geo-location data with records from the National Database and Registration Authority (NADRA), local police conducted arbitrary arrests of individuals deemed suspicious.

Non-State Actors

Non-state actors encompass private citizens affiliated with religious extremist groups, local drug cartels, and criminal organizations. These groups often target individuals based on their faith, religion, or involvement in covering stories on illegal activities. A significant incident highlighting this threat was the suicide bombing on January 30, 2023, which claimed numerous lives and instilled fear within the community. Examples of such attacks include the January 30, 2023 suicide bombing.

Hybrid Actors

Hybrid actors are typically corrupt government officials who exploit their positions to access and leak personal records of at-risk individuals. These officials may share sensitive information with private citizens or criminal groups seeking to surveil and monitor at-risk individuals, further endangering their safety.

At-Risk Individuals

At-risk individuals include journalists, activists, and human rights defenders whose work often involves reporting on issues that government agencies may wish to suppress. Protesters at rallies also fall into this category, as participation risks arrest.
